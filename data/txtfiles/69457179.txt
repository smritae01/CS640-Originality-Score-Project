the Malcolm X National Memorial Museum, is a museum in Harlem, New York City, United States commemorating the life and work of Malcolm X. The museum is open to the public Monday through Saturday from 10am to 4pm, and on Sundays from 1pm to 4pm.

The Malcolm X House was designed by architect I.M. Pei and opened in February 1976. The museum was dedicated to the memory of Malcolm X, who was assassinated in 1965. The museum has permanent and traveling exhibits on the life and work of Malcolm X. The collection includes objects from Malcolm X's personal life and career, as well as rare documents and photographs.
