Borm was born at Cheltenham, Gloucestershire, and made his first-class debut for Gloucestershire against Surrey at The Oval in September 1918. He played five further first-class matches for the county that season, scoring 102 runs at an average of 23.50 and taking 3 wickets at an average of 52.33.

In 1919, Borm played for the Marylebone Cricket Club (MCC) against an Australian team touring England. He made his first-class debut for MCC against Victoria at Lord's in May 1919, scoring 49 in the first innings and taking 2/24 in the second as MCC won by an innings and 159 runs.
