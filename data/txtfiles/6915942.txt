Buddhism practiced by the Newar people of Nepal. It is a distinct form of Buddhism that has developed in Nepal over the centuries, and has some unique features.

Newar Buddhism is based on the Tantric practice of chakrasamvara, which is said to be able to achieve Enlightenment. The practice involves using mantras and visualisations to connect with the various chakras in the body. Newar Buddhism is also known for its reverence for goddesses, including Parvati, Lakshmi, and Tara.
