Randolph was the son of John Randolph and Isabella Randolph. After studying law, he was admitted to the bar in 1871 and commenced practice in Lynchburg, Virginia. He served as prosecuting attorney for Clarke County from 1876 to 1880, and as city solicitor for Lynchburg from 1881 to 1884. In 1886, Randolph was elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-third Congress, serving from March 4, 1887 until his resignation on October 8, 1889. He then resumed the practice of law in Lynchburg. Randolph died there in 1918. He was interred in Elmwood Cemetery.
