Chavez was born in San Antonio, Texas, to parents of Mexican descent. He received his undergraduate degree from the University of Texas at Austin in 1951 and his doctorate from the University of California, Los Angeles in 1964.

Chavez began his career as a professor at UCLA before serving as the chancellor of the California State University system from 1978 to 1982. He later became a professor emeritus at UCLA. Chavez died of cancer in 1993.

Chavez's work has been influential in the field of Chicano studies and he has been described as "the father of Chicano studies". His books include: "The Chicano Experience" (1970), "Mesoamerican Voices" (1972), "The Mexican Border Trilogy" (1975), "The New Mexican Literary Tradition" (1980), "AngloAmericans" (1983), and "A History of the American People: The Hispanic Experience" (1984).
