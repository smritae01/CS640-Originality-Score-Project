Mianmin was born in 1797 in the town of Huai'an, Jiangsu province. He joined the army at an early age and rose through the ranks, becoming a general in 1841. In 1851, he participated in the suppression of the Taiping Rebellion and won distinction for his efforts.

In 1856, Mianmin was appointed governor of Sichuan province. He played a leading role in quelling the Taiping rebellion there and successfully defended Chengdu against a French invasion in 1860. In 1861, he was appointed commander-in-chief of the newly created Beiyang Army, which was later renamed the National Revolutionary Army. He played an important role in suppressing the Second Opium War (1856-1860).
