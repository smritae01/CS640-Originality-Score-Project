Claudio Lomnitz was born in Rome, Italy in 1962. He received his B.A. from the University of California at Berkeley in 1984 and his M.A. and Ph.D. from the University of Chicago in 1988 and 1992, respectively.

Lomnitz has been a professor at MIT since 1992. He has been the Campbell Family Professor of Economics since 2007. He is also a fellow of the Econometric Society, the American Academy of Arts and Sciences, and the National Academy of Sciences.

Lomnitz's research focuses on economic theory, game theory, and applied economics. His work has had a significant impact on both academic and industrial circles, with applications in areas such as business strategy, insurance, energy pricing, and telecommunications network design.
