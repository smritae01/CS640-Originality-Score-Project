Jean-Baptiste Bagaza was born in Bukavu on 29 August 1944. After completing his primary and secondary education in Bukavu, he studied at the University of Leuven, Belgium, where he obtained his doctorate in history in 1968. He returned to Congo to teach at the universities of Kinshasa and Lubumbashi. In 1977, he became director of research at the National Museum of the Congo. From 1992 to 1997, he served as the Minister of Culture and Communication in the government of Laurent Kabila. He was elected to the National Assembly of Congo in 2002 as a representative of the Union for Democracy and Social Progress (UDPS).

Bagaza was appointed Minister of Higher Education and Research in 2003 by President Joseph Kabila. He served as minister until his death on 4 May 2016.
