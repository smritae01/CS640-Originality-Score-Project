The Tumbledown red gum (Eucalyptus dealbata) is a medium-sized eucalyptus found in the North East Queensland region of Australia. It typically grows to a height of 8-12 m and has a rough, fibrous texture. The bark is red-brown in colour and covered in shallow fissures. The leaves are arranged in opposite pairs and are long and narrow, measuring up to 30 cm wide. The flowers are white and cone-shaped, and are produced in late winter or early spring. The fruit is a small, green pod which ripens in late summer or early autumn.
