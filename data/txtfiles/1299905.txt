The Bishop of Clogher is an episcopal title which takes its name from the town of Clogher in County Offaly, Ireland. The current bishop is Michael Cleary. The diocese of Clogher was created in 1838 by Pope Gregory XVI with the merger of the dioceses of Kilmore and Clonmacnoise. It covers an area of 1,827 square kilometres and has a population of 33,000. The bishop's residence is located in the town of Clogher.
