Born in late 1034 or early 1035, Constantine was the eldest son of John Doukas and his second wife Eudokia Makrembolitissa. Constantine was named after his paternal grandfather, Constantine X Doukas.

Constantine first appears in contemporary records as a minor nobleman who served as governor of the theme of Moesia Inferior in 1049â1051 and again from 1057 to 1059. He was promoted to protospatharios and served as governor of the Anatolic theme in 1059. In October 1059, he was named co-emperor with his father by Alexios I Komnenos, on the death of Michael IV Komnenos.

In 1070, Constantine married Eudokia Makrembolitissa, a niece of Emperor Romanos IV Diogenes. Together, they had three daughters: Anna (born c. 1070), Maria (born c. 1074), and Porphyra (born c. 1080).
