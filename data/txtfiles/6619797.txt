Manna was born in Brooklyn, New York, the son of a butcher. After serving in the United States Army during World War II, he began his music career in 1947 as a member of the Harry James Orchestra. In the early 1950s he formed his own trio and began recording for Capitol Records. Manna's biggest success came in the early 1960s with the song "The Boy from New York City". He had several other hits over the years, including "September Song", "I'm Waiting for You", and "The More I See You". Manna retired from performing in the late 1970s. He died in 1996 at the age of 78.
