Ratt began as a cover band in 1975 in San Diego, California. The original lineup included vocalist Stephen Pearcy, guitarists David Ellefson and Randy Castillo, bassist Dez Fafara, and drummer Robbin Crosby. The band signed with L.A. Rock Records in 1978 and released their debut album "Ratt" in 1980. The album reached number 41 on the "Billboard" 200 chart and was certified gold by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA).

The band's follow-up album, "Out of the Cellar", was released in 1981 and reached number 16 on the Billboard 200 chart. Their third album, "Infinite", was released in 1983 and reached number 10 on the Billboard 200 chart. The band's fourth album, "Evil Eye", was released in 1985 and reached number 12 on the Billboard 200 chart.
