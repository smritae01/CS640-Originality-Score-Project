Natalia Gvishiani was born on October 2, 1962 in Moscow, USSR. She began her studies at the Moscow State Linguistic University in 1981, majoring in Russian philology. In 1988 she earned her doctorate in Russian linguistics.

In 1991 Gvishiani emigrated to the United States and began working at the University of California, Berkeley as a linguistics lecturer. In 1995 she joined the faculty of the University of Pennsylvania, where she is currently a professor of Russian and East European studies.

Gvishiani's research focuses on Russian language and literature, with a particular focus on 19th century Russian literature. She has written extensively on Leo Tolstoy, Anton Chekhov, and Nikolai Gogol, among others. She has also published essays on Russian language education, contemporary Russian culture, and Slavic linguistics.

Gvishiani is the recipient of numerous awards and honors, including the Alexander Pushkin Prize (1998), the Ford Foundation Award for Distinguished Contributions to Scholarship (2002), and the Distinguished Faculty Award from the University of Pennsylvania (2008).
