Nash is best known for his work in game theory, economic philosophy, and mathematical economics. His best known result is the Nash equilibrium, a theoretical model of cooperation among players in a game. He also made significant contributions to the theory of probability and differential calculus.

Nash was born in 1910 in Hampstead, London, England to John Nash and Edith Howard. He was educated at Eton College and Trinity College, Cambridge, where he was awarded a Bachelor of Arts in 1931 and a Master of Arts in 1932. He then worked as a research fellow at Trinity College until 1936, when he moved to the University of Chicago to become a faculty member and professor of mathematics. In 1950 he returned to Cambridge to become the Lucasian Professor of Mathematics, a position he held until his retirement in 1971.

Nash died on 10 July 1994 at the age of 89.
