This owl is easily recognized by its large, round head, black facial mask, and brown body. It is the only species in its family, Tytonidae, and is the largest owl in North America. It typically weighs 2 to 3 pounds (1 to 1.5 kilograms). The greater sooty owl is a nocturnal hunter that feeds mainly on small animals such as rodents, birds, and insects.
