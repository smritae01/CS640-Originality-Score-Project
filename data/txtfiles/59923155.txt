Sugar Hill is a historically middle class and predominantly African American neighborhood in Jacksonville, Florida. It was established in the 1800s, was thriving by the end of the 19th century. It was largely destroyed by construction of the Jacksonville Expressway (now part of I-95), redevelopment plans including the expansion of a public hospital, as well as the end of segregation into the 1960s. History
 The community included professionals such as doctors, lawyers, teachers, builders, clergy, morticians, and railroad employees.
