(born December 2, 1948, in Paddington, London, England) is a British singer and songwriter.

Stevie Vallance began her career as the lead vocalist for the rock band The Four Seasons. After leaving The Four Seasons in 1973, Vallance embarked on a successful solo career. She has released numerous albums and singles, including the Top 10 hits "I Can't Give You Anything But Love" and "Sherry". Vallance has also been nominated for several Grammy Awards, including Best Female Pop Vocal Performance for "I Can't Give You Anything But Love".
