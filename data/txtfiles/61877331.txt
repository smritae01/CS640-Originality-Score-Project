Righetti was born in Milan to a family of modest origins. After studying law, he took a degree in economics at the University of Bologna, where he became known for his left-wing political views. In 1955, he began his career as a research economist at the Bank of Italy. He was appointed Deputy Governor of the Bank of Italy in 1969 and became Governor in 1972.

In 1976, Righetti was elected Prime Minister after the resignation of Aldo Moro. He served until 1978, when he was replaced by Giulio Andreotti. Righetti later served as Vice-President of the European Central Bank from 1984 to 1988.
