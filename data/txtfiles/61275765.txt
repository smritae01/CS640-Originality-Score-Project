Born in Tel Aviv to a family of Russian Jewish immigrants, Frumkin worked as a clerk in a grocery store before becoming a textile salesman. He joined the kibbutz Sha'ar HaGolan in 1942 and became active in its agricultural work. He also served as its chairman from 1951 until 1954.

Frumkin was elected to the Knesset on the Labor Party ticket in 1965, and became Minister of Agriculture in Menachem Begin's first government. He retained his ministerial position when Yitzhak Rabin formed his second government, and was also appointed Deputy Minister of Defense.

Frumkin lost his seat in the 1977 elections, but regained it in 1981. He retired from politics in 1984, but returned to serve as chairman of the Labor Party's election campaign committee. He died at the age of 86.
