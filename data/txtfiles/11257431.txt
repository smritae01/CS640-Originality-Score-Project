He was educated at Cheltenham College and Trinity College, Cambridge where he read archaeology. In 1892 he joined the British School of Archaeology in Egypt under the guidance of Flinders Petrie. Between 1896 and 1899, he undertook excavations at Deir el-Bahri.

In 1899, Theobald was appointed Keeper of the Department of Antiquities at the British Museum, a post he held until his retirement in 1937. During this time he organized and catalogued the Museum's collection of Ancient Egyptian art, making him one of the foremost experts on that subject. He also helped to develop methods for restoring damaged paintings.
